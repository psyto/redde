Solvency,
whether they
like it or not.

On-chain finance runs on self-attestation. A dashboard says 1:1. An audit says as of last quarter. Redde reconstructs the claim from mainnet state — without the protocol's consent, cooperation, or self-report — and renders a verdict every epoch.

The problem

Every number that matters reaches you as a claim.

Someone computed the peg, the backing, the collateral ratio — chose how to present it, and published it. The chain holds the truth; the dashboard holds the story. Most of the time the story is true. You have no way to know which time.

Audits are quarterly and consensual — the auditor is chosen and paid by the audited. Attestations are snapshots, signed by the party with the most to lose from an honest one. None of them is recomputed from the chain, right now, by someone who does not care whether the answer is convenient.

Exhibit A · JitoSOL · pool epoch 1004

The first target, recomputed from the accounts it controls.

JitoSOL is the largest Solana liquid-staking token. The public claim: every share is redeemable for its pro-rata amount of staked SOL. Redde did not ask Jito. It authenticated the pool against the canonical stake-pool program, derived the canonical PDA set from the validator list — reserve, every validator, every transient — and read those actual stake accounts straight off the Stake program. It found one further account pointing its withdrawer at the pool authority but absent from the list, and excluded it: a solvency proof counts only what the protocol's own accounting recognizes. Reproduce the verdict with one command and any RPC that serves getProgramAccounts.

Read with zero cooperation from Jito, at a single finalized slot, with a mutation guard over pool and validator list across the window. The withdraw authority comes from the stored bump; each validator and transient PDA from an on-curve program-address search; each account's usable state is verified; the reserve's rent-exempt reserve is excluded as non-redeemable. A GREEN means the claim survives an independent backing check — not a safety blessing. A RED means the chain contradicts the claim. A stale, mutating, or unreadable pool renders STALE, never GREEN. The stance

The verifier must be independent of the verified.

i

Solvency is a property of state, not of a press release.

If a protocol claims 1:1 backing, the accounts on mainnet either satisfy that claim or they do not. The verdict needs the chain and the arithmetic. Consent is not an input.

ii

When the same party makes the claim and grades it, there is no assurance — only a brand.

A prime broker running its own risk engine, a stablecoin reporting its own reserves, an L1 marketing its own health. The industry sells vertical integration as if the throat were not also the one making the promise. Independence is not inefficiency. It is the product.

iii

A claim that cannot be recomputed is already a finding.

When the backing is off-chain, the accounts opaque, the proof a signed JPEG — the verdict is not pass. It is STALE, and STALE is a color. Unverifiability is a property worth publishing.

How a verdict renders

Three colors. No fourth.

▣ Green

Every declared invariant holds. The claim survives recomputation against current mainnet state.

◆ Red

An invariant fails. The chain state contradicts the claim — reproducible by anyone with the spec.

▨ Stale

The claim cannot be recomputed now — opaque accounts, off-chain backing, dead RPC. Not a pass.

The honesty boundary

The weapon is only credible if it never fires on a target it cannot hit cleanly.

Redde publishes only what it can independently recompute from public claims and public state. It will not manufacture a RED for attention. It will not target private internals it has no public claim to check against. Restraint is what makes the RED, when it comes, undeniable.

The ask it makes of the industry is small and total: declare your invariant. State the property your users are trusting, in terms that can be recomputed from the chain. Then let it be recomputed, continuously, by someone you did not hire. Refusing to declare one is an answer too.
